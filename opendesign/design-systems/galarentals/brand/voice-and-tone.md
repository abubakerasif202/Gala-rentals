# Voice And Tone

## Public Voice

- Calm, premium, and practical.
- Lead with trust, clarity, and review-before-payment.
- Use Australian rental language: weekly rental, Sydney, bond, approved pricing, handover.
- Keep paragraphs short and specific.

Preferred phrases:

- "Apply online. Pay only after approval."
- "Secure Stripe checkout link"
- "Approved vehicle and weekly amount"
- "Sydney rental support"
- "Documents reviewed before payment"

Avoid:

- Marketplace language like "book instantly" when admin approval is required.
- Overpromising automatic handover after payment.
- Casual or cartoonish copy.

## Admin Voice

- Operational, exact, and auditable.
- Buttons should describe the outcome: "Approve and Send Payment Link", "Generate Agreement", "Mark Notice Sent".
- Error states should identify the blocked step, not blame the user.
- Audit and timeline copy should be terse and timestamp-oriented.

## Casing

- Public navigation: Title Case or short uppercase labels.
- Admin table headers: Title Case.
- Micro labels and badges: uppercase with wide tracking.
- Body copy: sentence case.

## Numbers

- Currency: `$420.00` when financial precision matters, `$420/wk` in summary cards.
- Dates: prefer Australian-readable formats in UI copy, keep exact dates in audit trails.
- Status labels: preserve existing values exactly: Pending, Approved, Rejected, Payment Review, Paid, Cancelled.
